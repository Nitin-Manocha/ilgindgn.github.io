About Me

I am a Ph.D. candidate in Industrial Engineering and Operations Research at the University of California, Berkeley (UCB). My academic advisors are Anil Aswani and Zuo-Jun Max Shen. Prior to joining UCB, I received B.S. and M.S. degrees in Industrial Engineering at the Middle East Technical University.

Research

My research focuses on designing adaptive approaches with strong theoretical and practical performance guarantees for sequential and data-driven decision-making processes. Motivated by complex and unexplored real-world operations management problems (ranging from supply chain to sustainability analytics), I exploit machinery from the methodologies of statistics, machine learning, optimization, and principal-agent theory.
